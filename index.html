  • Genre: Techno, Elektro

Biography

In 2010, 17-year-old Charlotte de Witte with dj's decided to use the artist name "Raving George" because this name meant she was a girl. She did not want to be judged on her appearance but on her dj skills. Charlotte de Witte began turning mainly electro and electrohouse music and was inspired by the music of The Bloody Beetroots, Brodinski and Crookers. After a few months, she decided to write herself for JIM's My Set Rules. A program of 4 young beginner DJs who play each other in the week by playing a set of fifteen minutes.

This was a great achievement in her career and made her famous. Throughout the years, De Wit started to play more and more Techno and less Electro. She also began to produce her own music. Her first EP Observe was released in 2013 and included 2 own songs and 3 remixes. In 2013 she also graduated in Event and Project Management at the Artevelde University College in Ghent.

Throughout the years, De Witte, under the name of Raving George, was more than Maal on Techno, Tomorrowland (5x), Pukkelpop (6x) and Wasserdag. She is also resident DJ at Studio Brussels in the Playground program.

Nina Kraviz

Information

  • Location: Irkoetsk, Rusland
  • Name: Nina Kravits
  • Genre: Dance, electronic music
  • Label: rekids, studio !k7

Biography

Kraviz worked in a number of roles before her music career, including fixing "cosmonauts" teeth, hosting a local Irkutsk radio show and writing for a fanzine. She was accepted to the Red Bull Music Academy in Seattle in 2005, but could not attend as she could not get a visa, attend the following year in Melbourne instead. By 2008 she was playing a regular night at the Propaganda Club in Moscow.

Kraviz released her self-titled debut album in February 2012 through the Rekid record label, to mixed-positive reviews. A video profile of Kraviz, Between The Beats: Nina Kraviz, was released in March 2013 and drew attention to her and triggered a discussion of feminism and sexuality in electronic music.

Kraviz started her own record label in 2014, named трип (English: Trip). She mixed the forty-eighth DJ-Kicks mix album, which was released in January 2015.

Dub fx

Information

  • Location: St.Kulda, Australia
  • Name: Benjamin Stanford
  • Genre: Reggae, dubstep
  • Label: Convoyen.LTD

Biography

Stanford used to travel and perform with his former fiancée, Flower Fairy. Dub FX first met her in Manchester, after which she joined him on his travels around the globe to busk for the public. Flower Fairy would usually be seen selling CDs during Dub FX's performances or on stage alongside Stanford when they perform several tracks together. She is also featured singing on tracks from Everythinks A Ripple, "Wandering Love", "Flow", and "Time Will Tell".

Two songs from Everythinks A Ripple, "Flow", and "Wandering Love", feature a street saxophonist named Mr. Woodnote, who also uses similar looping techniques as Dub FX, using a saxophone.

After his first summer traveling through Europe in 2006, he came home to Melbourne where he shared a flat with Melbourne-based producer Sirius. Stanford showed Sirius this new style from the UK called dubstep. and by 2010 together they released the album titled Dub FX and Sirius - A Crossworlds. Dub FX does not beatbox on this album.
